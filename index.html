Why governance fails

When governance lives in documents,
decisions fall between the gaps.

Every organization below had governance. Written policies, compliance frameworks, legal obligations, stated values. What they didn't have was a system that connected that governance to decisions being made every day. The gap between what was written and what actually happened cost them billions — and in most cases, it cost individuals their careers and freedom.

Wells Fargo
Sales conduct vs. stated values. Internal culture conflicted with the bank's own code of conduct for over a decade.
💰 Financial:$3 billion in fines. Federal Reserve asset cap still in force.
👤 Personal:CEO resigned, banned from banking for life, $69M clawed back. Retail banking chief: criminal conviction.
Boeing
Safety governance vs. competitive pressure. Production decisions never connected to safety commitments in real time.
💰 Financial:Over $20 billion — settlements, production caps, lost contracts.
👤 Personal:CEO fired with no severance. 346 people killed in two crashes.
Binance
Regulatory reporting. Systematically ignored AML and sanctions obligations.
💰 Financial:$4.3 billion — one of the largest corporate penalties in US history.
👤 Personal:CEO: criminal conviction, $50M personal fine, 4 months in prison.
Goldman Sachs
Regulatory reporting. Violated swap reporting rules four times in 18 months.
💰 Financial:$30M CFTC fine. Labelled 'a repeat federal defendant' by its own regulator.
👤 Personal:Significant reputational damage at board and executive level.

In every one of these cases, the policies existed. The frameworks existed. The problem was that governance lived in documents — and documents don't think, don't watch, and don't speak up at the moment a decision is being made. BonsAI was built to close this gap — not by adding more documents, but by making governance genuinely present in every decision, every day.

The thinking loop

Your governance, active across every function —
simultaneously.

Decisions in procurement, marketing, operations, and finance may all draw on the same governance — but when they're made by different people in different functions, with different perspectives and goals, in different moments, they almost inevitably lack visibility and alignment across functions. BonsAI closes that gap.

When your Risk & Compliance domain flags a regulatory tension, your Legal domain responds. Your Brand & Communications domain adds its perspective. This cross-functional governance conversation happens automatically, continuously, and every thought is stored, timestamped, and searchable.
