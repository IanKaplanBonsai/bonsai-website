Section 1

Why governance fails to reach the decisions that matter

Most organisations have more governance and guidance than they realise. Risk frameworks, compliance policies, legal guidelines, department and company-level goals, approval authorities, ethical commitments — years of careful work by compliance teams, legal counsel, strategic planners, and risk functions. The documents exist. The frameworks are sound. The intentions are genuine.

The problem is that governance lives in documents, and decisions happen in conversations, emails, spreadsheets, and meetings. The two rarely meet at the right moment. By the time someone checks whether a decision is aligned with policy — if they check at all — the decision has often already been made.

This is not a failure of intent. It is a structural problem. Governance is written once, stored centrally, and expected to reach thousands of decisions made by hundreds of people every day.

No policy document has ever read a news alert and flagged a regulatory risk. No compliance handbook has ever told a procurement manager that the supplier they are about to onboard conflicts with the company's ESG commitments. No legal framework has ever sent an alert before a marketing claim went out that needed review.

BonsAI is built on one principle: governance should be present at the moment of the decision — not reviewed after the damage is done.

📄
Governance is written and stored
Policies, frameworks, risk thresholds. Carefully crafted. Stored in a shared drive, a GRC system, or a document repository. Accessible to anyone — but only if they know to look.
⚡
Decisions happen continuously
Procurement, legal, finance, marketing, HR — decisions are made every day across every team. Most don't consult governance. Not because people are negligent, but because there is no mechanism that makes governance present at the right moment.
💥
The gap becomes visible — too late
A regulatory breach. A supplier that should not have been onboarded. A marketing claim that triggers a recall. In every post-mortem, the same finding: the policy existed, the information was available, but nobody connected them at the right moment.
✓
BonsAI closes the gap
Your governance, active at the moment of every relevant decision — before anything is executed.
Section 2

How BonsAI helps govern and align every decision in your organisation

BonsAI provides a dedicated governance function for each of your active domains — Risk & Compliance, Legal Exposure, Brand & Communications, Supplier & Sourcing, and more. Each function runs continuously and is configured entirely from your organisation's own governance.


Each domain function is configured with your organisation's own governance. There are no generic templates or industry averages — these are your policies, your risk thresholds, your decision authorities, your approval rules, your goals and guidelines. The specific commitments your organisation has made.

Once configured, each domain function runs continuously and independently. On a regular cycle, it:

  • Pulls relevant external signals: regulatory updates, industry news, and market developments relevant to its governance domain.
  • Reviews its own recent reasoning and the current state of your organisation's governance.
  • Generates a governance thought: structured reasoning that identifies tensions, risks, or required actions.
  • Shares that thought with peer domains, which read it and respond from their own governance perspective.

The result is a governance function that is always working, always current, and always grounded in what your organisation has actually committed to.

Section 3

How agents think together across governance domains

Every BonsAI governance agent runs a continuous thinking cycle throughout the day, whether your people are in the office or not.

On each cycle, the agent pulls real-time regulatory updates relevant to its domain, retrieves your organisation's governance context from memory, and generates a new structured thought — specific reasoning grounded in your actual policies against what is happening in the world right now.

After two weeks of running, your agents have built something no prompt can replicate. They have accumulated a working map of how your governance interacts with real-world dynamics, which tensions keep surfacing, and where your policies meet external pressure. When an employee asks a question, the agent has already been thinking about that domain for weeks.

Every thought, every inter-agent message, and every piece of reasoning is stored, timestamped, and searchable. If you need to show a regulator that your governance function was monitoring a specific risk during a specific period, the evidence is already there.

Section 4

How employees work inside BonsAI

Employees do their work inside BonsAI — building proposals, evaluating suppliers, drafting claims, planning decisions. They bring their work to the relevant governance domain and interact with it in plain language. There is no special syntax and no need to know which policy applies.

The domain responds with guidance grounded in your organisation's own governance. The response is:

  • Specific to the question asked — not a generic answer that could apply to any organisation.
  • Grounded in your actual governance — citing the relevant policy, rule, or threshold.
  • Clear about what to do next — whether that is proceeding, seeking approval, or stopping.
  • Honest about its limits — if human judgment is required, the agent says so and escalates.

Every interaction is logged automatically. The employee's name, the question, the guidance provided, the timestamp, and the governance sources cited are all recorded — without the employee doing anything.

Section 5

How your governance gets into BonsAI

Getting your governance into BonsAI is not a technology project. There is no data migration, no systems integration, and no requirement to restructure or rewrite your existing policies. It works in two ways.

1
Upload your existing documents
If you have policy documents, risk frameworks, compliance guidelines, or approval matrices, upload them. BonsAI reads them, processes the content, and embeds the governance into the relevant agent's knowledge. The agent starts reasoning from your documents immediately. No reformatting required.
2
Complete the onboarding conversation
A domain admin in your organisation has a structured conversation with the BonsAI onboarding agent. It asks a carefully designed sequence of questions covering the eight core areas of your governance for that domain: decision scope, failure modes, mandatory constraints, conditional allowances, escalation triggers, conflict resolution rules, approval authorities, and cross-domain interactions. The admin answers in plain language. The agent is configured from that conversation and is active immediately.
How long does onboarding take?
A single governance domain typically takes 30 to 60 minutes of onboarding conversation. You can onboard one domain and have the agent active the same day. Start with the domain where misaligned decisions are currently most costly. Add further domains as you see value. There is no big-bang implementation.

Section 6

Security and data architecture

Your governance data belongs to you. It is not shared with other organisations, not used to train models, and not accessible to BonsAI staff in the normal course of operations.

🔒Enterprise data isolation
Row-level security is enforced at the database layer. No enterprise can access another enterprise's governance data, agent thoughts, or employee queries. This is enforced by the database — it cannot be bypassed by application logic.
🏛Your governance stays yours
The governance you enter is stored in your enterprise's isolated environment. It is used exclusively to configure and run your agents. It is never used to train shared models or shared with other enterprises.
🗂Audit trail — permanent
Every employee question, agent response, escalation, and resolution is logged with a timestamp. This data is yours, accessible to your authorised administrators at any time, and exportable on request.
👤Role-based access control
Employees see only governance chat and agent intelligence. Enterprise admins see their enterprise's full dashboard. Platform-level access is restricted to BonsAI operators only. All enforced via Supabase Auth.
